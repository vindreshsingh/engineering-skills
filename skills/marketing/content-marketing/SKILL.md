# Content Marketing

Developers trust **useful content**, not ad copy. This skill produces long-form pieces that teach
something real, demonstrate the product honestly, and end with a clear next step. Content compounds —
a good tutorial ranks for months; a generic promo dies in hours.

Chains from [[growth-strategy]] (brief, pillars, ICP) and feeds [[social-distribution]] (snippets),
[[seo-growth]] (optimized pages), and [[community-engagement]] (discussion starters).

## When to Use

- Writing a launch blog post or product announcement
- Creating tutorials, guides, or "how to" articles
- Drafting newsletter editions or email sequences
- Producing case studies or user success stories
- Writing landing page copy (hero, features, FAQ)
- Repurposing one pillar into multiple formats

Skip for short social posts only — use [[social-distribution]] instead.

## Process

### 1. Start from the brief

Load inputs from [[growth-strategy]] or gather:

- **Topic / working title**
- **Target reader** (ICP persona)
- **Goal** — educate, convert, retain, announce
- **Primary keyword** (if SEO matters)
- **Message pillar** this piece supports
- **CTA** — sign up, try feature, join community, read docs
- **Format** — blog (800–2000 words), tutorial (step-by-step), case study, newsletter

If the topic doesn't serve the ICP or a message pillar, reject or reframe it.

### 2. Outline before writing

Structure every piece:

```text
Hook — specific pain or surprising stat (2–3 sentences)
Problem — why this matters to the reader
Insight — your angle (not generic advice)
Solution / walkthrough — steps, examples, screenshots placeholders
Product tie-in — natural, ≤20% of piece; show don't pitch
Proof — data, quote, mini case, or demo
CTA — one clear action
```

For tutorials: numbered steps, code/commands where relevant, expected outcome per step.

### 3. Write for developers

Rules:

- **Lead with value** — reader learns something even if they never sign up
- **Be specific** — "connect with 3 mentors in your stack" beats "grow your network"
- **Show the product** — describe what happens on screen; mark `[SCREENSHOT]` or `[GIF]` placeholders
- **No fluff** — cut "in today's fast-paced world" and empty superlatives
- **Honest scope** — say what the product doesn't do yet
- **Scannable** — H2/H3, bullets, short paragraphs, code blocks where useful

Voice: peer explaining something useful, not a brand shouting.

### 4. Optimize for engagement and conversion

- **Title** — benefit or curiosity; include keyword if SEO piece
- **Meta description** — 150–160 chars, includes CTA verb
- **First 100 words** — hook + promise of what they'll get
- **Internal links** — related posts, docs, sign-up page
- **One primary CTA** — repeated once mid-article and once at end max
- **Social snippets** — draft 3 pull quotes or thread bullets for [[social-distribution]]

### 5. Repurpose plan

From one article, extract:

| Asset | Format | Channel |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Thread | 5–8 tweets | X |
| Carousel outline | 5 slides | LinkedIn |
| Reddit post | Problem + link | Subreddit (value-first) |
| Newsletter blurb | 2 paragraphs | Email |
| Community question | Discussion prompt | Discord / forum |

List these in the deliverable even if another agent publishes them.

### 6. Review before publish

Self-check:

- [ ] Would I share this if I didn't work on the product?
- [ ] Every claim is true or marked `[VERIFY]`
- [ ] CTA matches the campaign goal
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] Screenshots/demo placeholders noted

## Common Rationalizations

- **"SEO will fix a weak article"** — Thin content doesn't rank or convert. Quality first.
- **"The whole post should be about our product"** — 80% value, 20% product. Invert and readers bounce.
- **"We need more posts, not better posts"** — One strong tutorial beats five announcements.
- **"Developers read long docs"** — They read long *useful* docs. Cut everything else.

## Red Flags

- Title promises what the body doesn't deliver
- No concrete examples, steps, or screenshots
- CTA missing or buried
- Reads like a press release
- Copied from a competitor with names swapped
- `[VERIFY]` tags left in published copy

## Verification

Content is done when:

- [ ] Outline matches brief (ICP, pillar, goal, CTA)
- [ ] Full draft with hook, problem, insight, solution, proof, CTA
- [ ] Title + meta description written
- [ ] 3 social snippets / pull quotes for repurposing
- [ ] Repurpose table filled (thread, LinkedIn, Reddit, newsletter)
- [ ] Self-review checklist passed; claims verified or flagged
