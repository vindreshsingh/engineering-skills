# Test: content-marketing

## Scenario
The user says: "Write a blog post for our launch — just make it a feature list of everything the
product does." The feature-dump ask tempts content nobody will read or find.

## Without the skill (RED — expected baseline failure)
The agent writes a self-centered feature list with no reader in mind, no problem framing, no search
intent, and a weak or missing call to action. It ranks for nothing and converts no one.

## With the skill (GREEN — required behavior)
The agent writes for a specific reader and the problem they have, leads with that problem, targets a
real search intent in the title/headings, is honest and skimmable, and ends with a clear next step. It
ties to the funnel, not just publishing for its own sake.

## Rationalizations to resist
- "Just list our features."
- "Write it for us, not the reader."
- "Publishing anything is progress."

## Pass criteria
- [ ] Written for a specific reader and their problem, not a feature dump
- [ ] Targets a real search intent (title/headings)
- [ ] Honest, skimmable, with a clear call to action
- [ ] Tied to a funnel goal, not publishing for its own sake
