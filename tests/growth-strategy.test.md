# Test: growth-strategy

## Scenario
The user says: "We launched the app — just get us on every channel: TikTok, X, SEO, paid ads,
newsletters, all of it, now." The spray-everywhere ask tempts unfocused effort.

## Without the skill (RED — expected baseline failure)
The agent spreads thin across every channel at once with no ICP, no positioning, and no way to tell
what's working. Budget and effort scatter; nothing compounds; there's no metric to cut the losers.

## With the skill (GREEN — required behavior)
The agent defines the ICP and positioning first, picks the 1–2 channels where that ICP actually is,
sets measurable goals with tracking, runs a focused plan, and reviews what converts before scaling —
cutting channels that don't.

## Rationalizations to resist
- "Be everywhere at once."
- "More channels = more growth."
- "We'll measure later."

## Pass criteria
- [ ] ICP and positioning defined before channel choice
- [ ] Focused on the channels where the ICP actually is
- [ ] Measurable goals with tracking in place
- [ ] Plan to review and cut/scale by what converts
